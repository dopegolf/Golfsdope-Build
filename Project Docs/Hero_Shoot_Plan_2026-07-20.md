## 3. Timing
**Recommended: wait for cooler season, shoot October through April.** Phoenix golden hour light is gentler and lasts longer, and it's dramatically more comfortable for talent, wardrobe (real apparel, not just shorts), and a crew standing around for setup. Early-to-mid October is the earliest the heat really breaks.
- Approximate sunset window early-to-mid October: roughly 6:15-6:25 PM local (Arizona doesn't observe daylight saving, so this holds steady). Golden hour = last ~45-60 minutes before sunset.
- Late October/November: sunset drifts earlier, to around 5:45-6:00 PM.

**If you want it sooner (ASAP option):** golden hour still exists in August, but daytime highs are commonly 105-115°F, so the whole shoot has to be compressed into the ~45 minutes around sunset (roughly 7:00-7:30 PM in early August) with talent arriving/staging in AC until go-time. More logistically demanding, more risk for anyone in full apparel.

Either way, confirm the exact sunset time for your chosen date 1-2 weeks out with a golden-hour app (PhotoPills, Golden Hour One, etc.) — don't lock the call sheet off the estimate above.

## 4. Talent
Recommended: **you and your circle** — Danielle, friends/family who golf — as the primary cast. It's real, it's authentic, and it's literally the story the brand brief says is "the engine." A hired model shoot would look more generic and cost more without adding anything the brand needs.
If you want more people in-frame for a "movement/group" feel than your immediate circle covers, supplementing with 2-3 local golfers (found through a course, a men's/women's league, or word of mouth) is a lighter lift than a full model booking.
